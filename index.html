Can AI find a way to project a 3D image?

Before I graduate from college, I want to find a way to make 3D images that you can see from many different directions. Today, there are a lot of ways to do just that, like holographic plates, 3D Glasses, Heads up displays, spinning mirrors, fog projections, and laser shows. There are also other interesting low tech optical illusions out there, like Pepper's Ghost, and mirascopes.

My idea is based on a mirascope.

To see how they work, click here!

I want to replace the top mirror and the object with an LED screen--allowing for animated, interactive objects. The problem? I need to direct the LEDs in specific directions, and I don't know what those directions are. Enter artificial intelligence.

One of the most basic forms of artificial intelligence is called hill climbing. In hill climbing, AI basically finds a solution, scores it according to some metric, and then tries to find a better one. After doing this for 'a long time', it finds a 'good' solution (although not necessarily the 'best' solution) based on your scoring metric.

I had two important 'scores' I wanted to improve: I wanted a lot of points in space to have light pass through them, and I wanted the light that passed through them to come from a lot of different angles. Although these aren't very specific metrics, they are enough that an AI can decide if one solution is better than another--if it has more points or more angles, then it is better!

Here is a display of what I have running so far: This screen replaces the upper screen of the mirascope, and the colors represent directions the LEDs are pointing. More red means the LED's are pointing toward the right.More Green means they are Pointing toward the bottom of the screen, and More blue means they are pointing directly into the screen, straight toward the mirror (you'll have to imagine the mirror.)
